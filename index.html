Refractor Telescopes

Refractor telescopes are the classic "pirate" telescope design, with the eyepiece at the bottom of the telescope body. Refractors are sturdy and reliable due to only containing lenses inside a sealed, dust-proof tube.

Reflector Telescopes

Reflector, or Newtonian, telescopes contain a large parabolic mirror at the base of the telescope body which reflects light back upwards to a smaller mirror which is then observed through the eyepiece at the open top end of the tube. Reflector telescopes are cheaper relative to similar diameter refractors but are prone to mirror misalignment and require regular cleaning due to their open telescope body.

Dobsonian Telescopes

Dobsonian telescopes are essentially reflector telescopes mounted on a large box-like mount and are the easiest to set up and use. They are ideal for deep-space observations and for maximum aperture-to-price value. However, Dobsonians are bulky, not very portable and not suitable for astrophotography.
